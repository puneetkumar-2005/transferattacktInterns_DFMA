1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
This report details the evaluation of the Dynamic Feature-Mixing Attack (D-FMA) 
on the docs/subset_input_pairs.csv subset. D-FMA operates by isolating and 
blending the highly discriminative periocular and nasal regions using a 
Gaussian-blurred static mask prior to MI-FGSM optimization. 

The attack was generated across four distinct surrogate architectures 
(Facenet512, ArcFace, GhostFaceNet, VGG-Face) and evaluated for cross-model 
transferability. D-FMA achieved an average transfer breach rate of 59.17%, 
outperforming the top baseline (SI-NI-FGSM) by exactly 30.00%.

4. METHODOLOGICAL ADVANTAGE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
The standard baselines (PGD, MI-FGSM, etc.) rely on global pixel-level noise 
that frequently overfits to the surrogate model's spatial layout. By contrast, 
D-FMA physically alters the fundamental semantic geometry of the face before 
optimization. By forcing the victim model to evaluate conflicting spatial 
anchors (e.g., Target identity's eyes mathematically blended into the Source 
identity's facial structure), D-FMA successfully bypassed the robust filtering 
defenses of the victim architectures, resulting in a >2x increase in 
transferability over standard MI-FGSM.
